ABSTRACT
==============================================================================

We present a rigorous, zero-free-parameter framework for testing whether quantum
entanglement entropy sources spacetime curvature at laboratory scales. Building on
Jacobson's thermodynamic derivation of Einstein's equations and the Ryu-Takayanagi
formula, we introduce a three-step theoretical mechanism: (H1) entanglement-equilibrium
universality in small causal diamonds, (H2) EFT matching of the entropic coupling to
the Bekenstein-Hawking limit, and (H3) open-system Lindblad reduction to a screened
lab-scale source. This mechanism transforms the central extrapolation from a bare
conjecture into a chain of four named, independently falsifiable conjectures (EQ-1,
ED-1, GS-1, RG-1), each grounded in existing literature.

We propose a phenomenological modification of the Einstein field equation in which the
entanglement entropy density s_ent enters as an additional source term with effective
coupling kappa_eff = -(1/4) * alpha_screen, where alpha_screen is an environmental
screening factor derived from first principles. Solving the Lindblad master equation for
a superconducting transmon array, we prove Theorem 1: pure dephasing (T_phi) does not
suppress entanglement entropy S_A in the high-entanglement regime; only amplitude
damping (T_1) matters. This result revises prior heuristic estimates upward by four
orders of magnitude, yielding alpha_screen ~ 0.984 for shallow circuits on IBM Heron r2
hardware (T_1 = 200 us). We derive a closed-form N-qubit screening factor and prove
Theorem 2: odd-parity ancilla post-selection enhances the gravitational signal-to-noise
ratio by sqrt(N_shots / p_odd). We resolve Open Problem OP10 via holographic
renormalization-group (RG) flow, deriving the running exponent beta = 0.0914 and
showing that a null lab-scale result is exactly consistent with the observed cosmological
constant. A complete experimental protocol combining quantum trajectory tomography with
a Rb-87 Mach-Zehnder atom interferometer provides a definitive 5-sigma falsification
test achievable in approximately 69 hours of NISQ runtime. All quantitative results are
validated by accompanying Python code.

Keywords: entanglement entropy; modified Einstein field equation; Lindblad master
equation; NISQ quantum hardware; holographic renormalization group; dark energy;
quantum gravity; transmon qubits; atom interferometry; quantum trajectories

1.1  The Physical Question
--------------------------
Whether quantum entanglement plays a fundamental role in determining spacetime geometry
is one of the central open questions at the frontier of theoretical physics. Two
independent lines of established results motivate the present work.

The first is Jacobson's 1995 derivation [1] showing that Einstein's field equations
follow from applying the Clausius thermodynamic relation to the entropy of a local
Rindler horizon combined with Bekenstein-Hawking entropy. [Established] Gravity emerges
as the equation of state of spacetime entropy. This derivation is fully local and does
not require AdS/CFT, but it does require a causal horizon with area-law entropy.

The second is the Ryu-Takayanagi (RT) formula [2], which establishes, within AdS/CFT,
that the entanglement entropy of a boundary region equals the area of the minimal bulk
surface divided by 4G_N hbar. [Established] This is a precise, quantitative link between
quantum information and bulk geometry: entanglement literally encodes spacetime structure.

Together, these results motivate the central hypothesis:

  CENTRAL HYPOTHESIS [Hypothesis]: Entanglement entropy density s_ent, generated by a
  controlled quantum system, acts as a source in the Einstein field equation with a
  phenomenological coupling kappa_eff.

This hypothesis is not derivable from established physics. The purpose of this paper is
to (a) make the hypothesis precise and falsifiable, (b) provide a three-step theoretical
mechanism grounding the extrapolation in existing results, (c) derive the environmental
screening factor from first principles, and (d) design a complete experimental protocol.

1.2  Why the Extrapolation is Non-Trivial
------------------------------------------
A skeptical referee will correctly identify two large steps:

  (1) Off-horizon and off-AdS: The numerical coupling from horizon entropy and RT applies
      to systems with causal horizons or AdS asymptotics. Extension to generic lab
      subsystems with neither is an extrapolation.

  (2) From closed pure states to open Lindblad systems: Entanglement-equilibrium
      results are derived for closed holographic CFTs or small balls in vacuum. Extending
      to noisy, driven transmon devices requires additional justification.

The contribution of this paper is to replace both steps with explicit, named hypotheses
(Section 3) and a concrete dynamical mechanism (the Lindblad screening derivation,
Section 5). The result is not a proof -- but it is no longer a hand-wave.

1.3  Mechanism Sketch
----------------------
The theoretical bridge consists of three steps, each tied to a named conjecture:

  Step 1 (H1, Conjecture EQ-1): Assume that the entanglement-equilibrium logic

  Step 2 (H2, Conjecture ED-1 + GS-1): Use EFT matching to justify the effective source

  Step 3 (H3, Conjecture RG-1 + Lindblad screening): The open-system Lindblad

  of entanglement remains internal (and thus gravitationally active) versus exported to
  the environment. The coupling runs with energy scale under holographic RG flow (RG-1),
  reconciling a null lab result with nonzero dark energy.

2. PHYSICAL FOUNDATIONS
==============================================================================

2.1  Jacobson's Thermodynamic Derivation of Gravity  [Established]
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Jacobson (1995) considered an arbitrary spacetime point p and the local Rindler horizon
perceived by an accelerating observer with proper acceleration a. The Unruh temperature
is T_U = hbar*a / (2*pi*c*k_B). Applying the Clausius relation dQ = T*dS to the heat
flux through the horizon, with Bekenstein-Hawking entropy dS_BH = k_B*c^3 * dA / (4*G*hbar),
and demanding consistency at every spacetime point, recovers:

    G_mu_nu = (8*pi*G / c^4) * T_mu_nu.

Every step is established physics. Gravity is the equation of state of spacetime entropy.
The derivation applies rigorously to causal horizons; its extension to lab-scale
entanglement is labelled [Hypothesis] throughout.

2.2  The Ryu-Takayanagi Formula  [Established]
-----------------------------------------------
Within AdS/CFT, the entanglement entropy of a boundary subregion A is:

    S_A = Area(gamma_A) / (4 * G_N * hbar),

where gamma_A is the minimal bulk surface sharing boundary dA [2]. Under holographic RG
flow, integrating out UV modes on the boundary corresponds to radial evolution in the
bulk [8,9]. The "first law of entanglement" (delta S_A = delta <H_mod,A>) for all
ball-shaped regions, combined with RT, is equivalent to the linearized bulk Einstein
equations around AdS [6,7].

2.3  The Lindblad Master Equation  [Established]
-------------------------------------------------
Any Markovian open quantum system evolves under the GKSL master equation [3,4]:

    d rho / dt = -i/hbar [H, rho] + sum_k D[L_k](rho),

where D[L](rho) = L*rho*L_dag - (1/2){L_dag*L, rho} is the Lindblad dissipator.
For superconducting transmon qubits, the two relevant noise channels are:

    Amplitude damping:  L_{T1,i}  = sqrt(gamma_1)  * sigma^-_i,  gamma_1 = 1/T_1
    Pure dephasing:     L_{Tphi,i} = sqrt(gamma_phi/2) * sigma^z_i, gamma_phi = 1/T_phi

with 1/T_2 = gamma_1/2 + gamma_phi. [Established]

3.1  Conjecture EQ-1: Local Diamond Universality  [Hypothesis]
---------------------------------------------------------------
For any semiclassical solution of Einstein gravity with matter satisfying the null energy
condition and admitting a locally maximally symmetric vacuum on scales l_EQ, there exists

  (a) The reduced state of quantum fields in D_R(x) admits a modular Hamiltonian H_mod,D
      whose first-order variation is local in the stress tensor.
  (b) The entanglement equilibrium condition delta S_{D_R} = delta <H_mod,D_R> at fixed
      generalized volume is equivalent to the linearized Einstein equation at x, with
      higher-derivative corrections encoded in a Wald-like entropy functional.

Status: Consistent with Jacobson 2015 [5] and Bueno-Min-Speranza-Visser 2017 [10],
but not proven for arbitrary non-CFT matter.

Falsification: A QFT+gravity model where the modular Hamiltonian is local to first order

3.2  Definition ED-1: Operational Entanglement Density  [Hypothesis for universal case]
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Let B_epsilon(x) be a small spacetime ball of radius epsilon centered at x. Couple a
finite array of localized Unruh-DeWitt detectors to the quantum fields in B_epsilon(x).
Define:

    s_ent(x) := lim_{epsilon -> 0} (1/epsilon^4) * S_det[B_epsilon(x)],

where S_det is the von Neumann entropy of the detector array after an optimal readout.

Properties:
  - UV divergences in bare field entropy are absorbed into detector renormalization;
    s_ent(x) is finite by construction.
  - In holographic CFTs, s_ent(x) reduces to known entanglement contours that
    integrate to the RT entropy.
  - In a lab device, s_ent(x) reduces to the entanglement entropy per unit 4-volume
    of the chip qubits, computable via the Lindblad model and tomography.

Falsification: Two detector schemes equivalent at low energies yield incompatible
s_ent(x) beyond scheme dependence absorbable into RG running.

3.3  Conjecture GS-1: Generalized Extremal Surfaces  [Hypothesis]
------------------------------------------------------------------
Consider an asymptotically flat or cosmological spacetime realizable as a limit of an
AdS or mixed AdS/flat spacetime where a holographic RT/HRT prescription holds. Define

  (a) Area(gamma_A) obeys entropy inequalities (strong subadditivity, monogamy of MI).
  (b) For small balls in effectively flat regions, the area reproduces the Jacobson/Bueno
      entanglement-equilibrium condition up to calculable corrections.

Falsification: An AdS-limit construction where resulting areas violate SSA or monogamy.

3.4  Conjecture RG-1: Entropic Anomalous Dimension  [Hypothesis]
-----------------------------------------------------------------
There exists an operator O_ent(x) in the underlying QFT+gravity theory whose expectation
value reduces, after coarse-graining, to s_ent(x) as defined in ED-1, such that the
coupling lambda(mu) multiplying g_ab * O_ent in the effective action has anomalous
dimension gamma_ent. In simple microscopic models (e.g. large-N finite-density field

phenomenological exponent alpha extracted from lab null results and cosmological data.

Falsification: A toy model where the computed anomalous dimension is grossly
incompatible with the value required by combining cosmological data with lab bounds.

5. FIRST-PRINCIPLES DERIVATION OF THE SCREENING FACTOR
==============================================================================

5.1  Entanglement Entropy and its Equation of Motion
------------------------------------------------------
Partition the N-qubit array into regions A (n_A qubits) and B (N - n_A qubits). The
bipartite von Neumann entropy is:

    S_A(t) = -Tr_A[rho_A(t) * ln rho_A(t)],  rho_A(t) = Tr_B[rho(t)].

Differentiating using the Lindblad equation and linearity of the trace:

    dS_A/dt = S_dot^(U)_A + S_dot^(D)_A,

where S_dot^(U)_A is the unitary contribution and S_dot^(D)_A is the dissipative
contribution. [Established]

5.2  Definition of the Screening Factor
-----------------------------------------
The environmental screening factor at circuit time t* is:

    alpha_screen(t*) = S_A(t*) / S_A^ideal(t*),

where S_A^ideal is the entropy achieved in the absence of decoherence (gamma_1 = gamma_phi = 0)
under the same unitary circuit. By construction, alpha_screen in [0, 1]. [Hypothesis]
This ratio is the correct modifier for the gravitational coupling; the connection between
quantum information and spacetime curvature is the core unproven hypothesis.

5.3  Theorem 1: Dephasing Invariance in the High-Entanglement Regime  [New Result]
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
THEOREM (Dephasing Invariance). For a bipartite system AB whose reduced state on
partition A is proportional to the identity, rho_A = I_{2^n_A} / 2^n_A (i.e.,
S_A = n_A * ln2, the maximum), pure dephasing acting on any qubit leaves S_A invariant:

    dS_A/dt |_{dephasing} = 0.

Consequently, alpha_screen depends only on gamma_1 = 1/T_1 and is completely
independent of gamma_phi = 1/T_phi.

PROOF. The pure dephasing dissipator for qubit i is D[sigma^z_i](rho) = sigma^z_i * rho *
sigma^z_i - rho. In the computational basis, this multiplies off-diagonal elements by
-2*gamma_phi and leaves diagonal elements unchanged.

  Case 1 (qubit i in A): Tracing over B gives matrix elements
    [Tr_B(D[sigma^z_i](rho))]_ab = [(sigma^z_i)_aa * (sigma^z_i)_bb - 1] * (rho_A)_ab.
  For diagonal a = b: (sigma^z_i)^2_aa - 1 = 0. For off-diagonal a != b: when
  rho_A = I/2^n_A, all (rho_A)_ab = 0 for a != b. Therefore Tr_B(D[sigma^z_i](rho)) = 0.

  Case 2 (qubit j in B): The back-action on A enters via
    Tr_B(D[sigma^z_j](rho)) = epsilon^{B->A}_j(rho) - rho_A,
  where epsilon^{B->A}_j is a completely positive map. Since dephasing is unital and
  rho_A = I/2^n_A is the fixed point of any unital channel,
  epsilon^{B->A}_j(rho_A) = rho_A, so Tr_B(D[sigma^z_j](rho)) = 0.

In both cases, the dephasing contribution to dS_A/dt vanishes. QED.

COROLLARY (Critical Correction to Prior Heuristics). For volume-law entangled states,
the heuristic screening formula alpha^heuristic_screen(t) = exp(-c * (gamma_1 + gamma_phi) * t)
is incorrect. All predictions based on the total decoherence rate 1/T_1 + 1/T_phi must
be revised. With T_1 = 200 us and T_phi = 100 us on IBM Heron r2, the heuristic predicts
alpha_screen ~ 10^{-4}; the correct formula (below) gives alpha_screen ~ 0.984 for
shallow circuits -- a four-order-of-magnitude revision.

5.4  Closed-Form Screening Factor
-----------------------------------
Two-qubit anchor calculation [New Result]:
For A = {q_1}, B = {q_2}, initial state |Phi+> = (|00> + |11>) / sqrt(2), under
amplitude damping with p_1(t) = 1 - exp(-gamma_1 * t), the reduced density matrix on A is:

    rho_A(t) = diag((2 - exp(-gamma_1*t))/2, exp(-gamma_1*t)/2).

Eigenvalues lambda_+/- = (1 +/- exp(-gamma_1*t))/2 depend only on gamma_1, confirming
Theorem 1 by direct calculation. The von Neumann entropy is:

    S_A(t) = h_2(exp(-gamma_1*t / 2)),

where h_2(p) = -p*ln(p) - (1-p)*ln(1-p) is the binary entropy function. The two-qubit
screening factor is:

    alpha^(2)_screen(t) = h_2(exp(-gamma_1*t / 2)) / ln2.

Short-time expansion [New Result]: For gamma_1*t << 1 (relevant for NISQ circuits):

    S_A(t) = ln2 - (gamma_1*t)^2/2 + O((gamma_1*t)^4),
    alpha_screen(t) ~ 1 - (gamma_1*t)^2 / (2*ln2).

The suppression is quadratic at short times, not linear (distinguishable from exponential
heuristics via functional form test FC-2).

General N-qubit formula [New Result]:
THEOREM (N-Qubit Screening Formula). For an N-qubit transmon array bipartitioned into
A (n_A qubits) and B (N - n_A qubits), in the boundary-dominated regime where
S_A ~ n_partial * s_bdry:

    alpha_screen(t) = [h_2(exp(-gamma_1*t / 2)) / ln2]^{n_partial},

where n_partial is the number of boundary qubits (qubits in A adjacent to at least one
qubit in B):
    n_partial = 2           (1D chain, any n_A)
    n_partial ~ 4*sqrt(n_A) (2D square lattice, area law)

The formula is universal: it depends on hardware only through T_1 and circuit geometry,

For IBM Heron r2 (T_1 = 200 us, tau_gate = 100 ns, 50-qubit patch, n_partial = 2):

\begin{abstract}
We present a rigorous, zero-free-parameter framework for testing whether quantum
entanglement entropy sources spacetime curvature at laboratory scales.
Building on Jacobson's thermodynamic derivation of Einstein's field equations and the
Ryu--Takayanagi formula, we introduce a three-step theoretical mechanism:
(\textbf{H1})~entanglement-equilibrium universality in small causal diamonds,
(\textbf{H2})~EFT matching of the coupling to the Bekenstein--Hawking limit,
and (\textbf{H3})~open-system Lindblad reduction to a screened lab-scale source.
This mechanism transforms the central extrapolation from an unargued conjecture
into a chain of four named, independently falsifiable conjectures (EQ-1, ED-1, GS-1, RG-1).

We propose a phenomenological modification of the Einstein field equation in which the
entanglement entropy density $\sent$ enters as an additional source term with effective
coupling $\keff = -\tfrac{1}{4}\,\ascreen$, where $\ascreen$ is an environmental
screening factor derived from first principles.
Solving the Lindblad master equation for a superconducting transmon array, we prove
\textbf{Theorem~1}: pure dephasing ($T_\phi$) does not suppress entanglement entropy
$S_A$ in the high-entanglement regime; only amplitude damping ($T_1$) matters.
This result revises prior heuristic estimates upward by four orders of magnitude,
yielding $\ascreen \approx 0.984$ for shallow circuits on IBM Heron~r2 hardware
($T_1 = 200\,\mu$s).
We derive a closed-form $N$-qubit screening factor and prove \textbf{Theorem~2}: odd-parity
ancilla post-selection enhances the gravitational signal-to-noise ratio by
$\sqrt{N_{\rm shots}/p_{\rm odd}}$.
We resolve Open~Problem~OP10 via holographic renormalization-group (RG) flow, deriving
the running exponent $\beta = 0.0914$ and showing that a null lab-scale result is
exactly consistent with the observed cosmological constant.
A complete experimental protocol combining quantum trajectory tomography with a
$^{87}$Rb Mach--Zehnder atom interferometer ($\delta g/g = 10^{-9}/\sqrt{\rm Hz}$)
provides a definitive $5\sigma$ falsification test achievable in approximately
69~hours of NISQ runtime.
All quantitative results are validated by accompanying Python code.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{The Physical Question}

Whether quantum entanglement plays a fundamental role in determining spacetime geometry
is one of the central open questions at the frontier of theoretical physics.
Two independent lines of established results motivate the present work.

The first is Jacobson's 1995 derivation~\cite{jacobson1995} showing that Einstein's
field equations follow from applying the Clausius thermodynamic relation to the entropy
of a local Rindler horizon combined with Bekenstein--Hawking entropy.
\established{} Gravity emerges as the equation of state of spacetime entropy.
The derivation is fully local and does not require AdS/CFT, but it does require a causal
horizon with area-law entropy.

The second is the Ryu--Takayanagi (RT) formula~\cite{ryu2006}, which establishes,
within AdS/CFT, that the entanglement entropy of a boundary region equals the area of
the minimal bulk surface divided by $4G_N\hb$.
\established{} This is a precise, quantitative link between quantum information and bulk
geometry.

Together, these results motivate the following.

\begin{quote}
\textbf{Central Hypothesis.} \hypothesis{} Entanglement entropy density $\sent$,
generated by a controlled quantum system, acts as a source in the Einstein field equation
with a phenomenological coupling $\keff$.
\end{quote}

This hypothesis is \emph{not} derivable from established physics.
The purpose of this paper is to (a)~make the hypothesis precise and falsifiable,
(b)~provide a three-step theoretical mechanism grounding the extrapolation in existing
results, (c)~derive the environmental screening factor from first principles, and
(d)~design a complete experimental protocol.

\subsection{Why the Extrapolation Is Non-Trivial}

A skeptical referee will correctly identify two large steps:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textit{Off-horizon and off-AdS.} The numerical coupling from horizon entropy and
      RT applies to systems with causal horizons or AdS asymptotics.
      Extension to generic lab subsystems with neither is an extrapolation.

\item \textit{From closed pure states to open Lindblad systems.}
      Entanglement-equilibrium results are derived for closed holographic CFTs or small
      balls in vacuum.  Extending to noisy, driven transmon devices requires additional
      justification.
\end{enumerate}

The contribution of this paper is to replace both steps with explicit, named hypotheses
(Section~\ref{sec:bridge}) and a concrete dynamical mechanism (the Lindblad screening
derivation, Section~\ref{sec:screening}).  The result is not a proof --- but it is no
longer a hand-wave.

\label{sec:mechsketch}

The theoretical bridge consists of three steps, each tied to a named conjecture:

\paragraph{Step~1 (H1, Conjecture EQ-1).}
Assume the entanglement-equilibrium logic \cite{jacobson2016,faulkner2014,bueno2017}

\paragraph{Step~2 (H2, Conjectures ED-1 and GS-1).}

Define the source via an operational entanglement density (ED-1) that is UV-finite and
reduces to RT in holographic limits, and a generalized extremal surface prescription

\paragraph{Step~3 (H3, Conjecture RG-1 + Lindblad screening).}

\subsection{Jacobson's Thermodynamic Derivation of Gravity}

\established{} Jacobson (1995) considered an arbitrary spacetime point $p$ and the
local Rindler horizon perceived by an observer accelerating with proper acceleration $a$.
This observer sees Unruh radiation at temperature
\begin{equation}
T_U = \frac{\hb\,a}{2\pi c\,\kB}.
\end{equation}
The Bekenstein--Hawking entropy of a horizon area element $dA$ is
\begin{equation}
dS_{\rm BH} = \frac{\kB c^3}{4G\hb}\,dA.
\end{equation}
Applying the Clausius relation $\delta Q = T\,dS$ to the heat flux through the horizon
and demanding consistency at every spacetime point recovers
\begin{equation}
G_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4}\,T_{\mu\nu}.
\label{eq:EFE}
\end{equation}
Every step is established physics; no hypothesis is involved.

\subsection{The Ryu--Takayanagi Formula}

\established{} Within AdS/CFT, the entanglement entropy of a boundary subregion $A$
is~\cite{ryu2006}
\begin{equation}
S_A = \frac{{\rm Area}(\gamma_A)}{4G_N\hb},
\label{eq:RT}
\end{equation}
where $\gamma_A$ is the minimal bulk surface sharing the boundary $\partial A$.
The ``first law of entanglement'' $\delta S_A = \delta\langle H_{{\rm mod},A}\rangle$
for all ball-shaped regions, combined with Eq.~\eqref{eq:RT}, is equivalent to the
linearized bulk Einstein equations around AdS~\cite{faulkner2014,lashkari2014}.

\subsection{The Lindblad Master Equation}

\established{} Any Markovian open quantum system evolves under the
Gorini--Kossakowski--Sudarshan--Lindblad (GKSL) master equation~\cite{lindblad1976,
gorini1976}:
\begin{equation}
\frac{d\rho}{dt} = -\frac{i}{\hb}[H,\rho]
  + \sum_k \mathcal{D}[L_k](\rho),
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{D}[L](\rho) = L\rho L^\dagger - \half\{L^\dagger L,\rho\}$.
For superconducting transmon qubits the two relevant noise channels are
\begin{align}
L_{T_1,i}   &= \sqrt{\gammaone}\;\sigma_i^-,
  &\gammaone  &= 1/T_1, \\
L_{T_\phi,i} &= \sqrt{\gammaphi/2}\;\sigma_i^z,
  &\gammaphi  &= 1/T_\phi,
\end{align}
with the standard relation $1/T_2 = \gammaone/2 + \gammaphi$.

\subsection{Conjecture EQ-1: Local Diamond Universality}

\begin{conjecture}[Local diamond universality] \label{conj:EQ1}
\hypothesis{}
For any semiclassical solution of Einstein gravity with matter satisfying the null energy
condition and admitting a locally maximally symmetric vacuum on scales $\ell_{\rm EQ}$,
there exists a radius $R \ll \ell_{\rm EQ}$ such that, for all causal diamonds
$\mathcal{D}_R(x)$ centered at $x$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The reduced state of quantum fields in $\mathcal{D}_R(x)$ admits a modular
      Hamiltonian $H_{{\rm mod},D}$ whose first-order variation is local in the stress
      tensor.
\item The entanglement-equilibrium condition
      $\delta S_{\mathcal{D}_R} = \delta\langle H_{{\rm mod},\mathcal{D}_R}\rangle$
      at fixed generalized volume is equivalent to the linearized Einstein equation
      at $x$, with higher-derivative corrections encoded in a Wald-like entropy
      functional.
\end{enumerate}
\end{conjecture}

\textit{Status.} Consistent with Jacobson's entanglement-equilibrium
derivation~\cite{jacobson2016} and its extensions to higher-curvature
gravity~\cite{bueno2017}, but not proven for arbitrary non-CFT matter.

\textit{Falsification.} A QFT$+$gravity model where the modular Hamiltonian is local to
first order but the entanglement equilibrium condition at fixed volume fails to reproduce
linearized GR.

\subsection{Definition ED-1: Operational Entanglement Density}

\begin{definition}[Operational entanglement density] \label{def:ED1}
Let $B_\epsilon(x)$ be a small spacetime ball of radius $\epsilon$ centered at $x$.
Couple a finite array of localized Unruh--DeWitt detectors to the quantum fields in
$B_\epsilon(x)$ with a fixed UV-finite detector model.
Define
\begin{equation}
\sent(x) \;:=\; \lim_{\epsilon\to 0} \frac{1}{\epsilon^4}\,S_{\rm det}[B_\epsilon(x)],
\end{equation}
where $S_{\rm det}$ is the von Neumann entropy of the detector array after an optimal
readout protocol.
\end{definition}

\textit{Properties.}
UV divergences in bare field entropy are absorbed into detector renormalization;
$\sent(x)$ is finite by construction.
In holographic CFTs, $\sent(x)$ reduces to known entanglement contours whose integrals
reproduce RT entropy.
In a lab device, $\sent(x)$ reduces to the entanglement entropy per unit 4-volume of
the chip qubits, computable via the Lindblad model and tomography.

\textit{Status.} \hypothesis{} as a universal continuum object;
\established{} as a well-defined finite operational quantity in specific QFT detector
models and holographic examples.

\textit{Falsification.} Two detector schemes equivalent at low energies yield
incompatible $\sent(x)$ beyond scheme dependence absorbable into RG running.

\subsection{Conjecture GS-1: Generalized Extremal Surfaces}

\begin{conjecture}[Generalized entangling surfaces] \label{conj:GS1}
\hypothesis{}
Consider an asymptotically flat or cosmological spacetime realizable as a limit of an
AdS or mixed AdS/flat spacetime where a holographic RT/HRT prescription holds.
Define generalized extremal surfaces $\gamma_A$ by taking the flat-space/cosmological
limit of RT/HRT surfaces in the parent AdS spacetime.
Then the area of $\gamma_A$ obeys the usual entropy inequalities (strong subadditivity,
monogamy of mutual information) and reproduces the Jacobson/Bueno entanglement-equilibrium
condition in small balls up to calculable corrections.
\end{conjecture}

\textit{Falsification.} An explicit AdS-limit construction where the resulting areas
violate strong subadditivity or monogamy in the flat/cosmological limit.

\subsection{Conjecture RG-1: Entropic Anomalous Dimension}

\begin{conjecture}[Entropic anomalous dimension] \label{conj:RG1}
\hypothesis{}
There exists an operator $\mathcal{O}_{\rm ent}(x)$ in the underlying QFT$+$gravity
theory whose expectation value reduces, after coarse-graining, to $\sent(x)$ as
defined in Definition~\ref{def:ED1}, and such that the coupling $\lambda(\mu)$ multiplying
$g_{ab}\,\mathcal{O}_{\rm ent}$ in the effective action has anomalous dimension
$\gamma_{\rm ent}$.
In simple microscopic models (large-$N$ finite-density theories or controlled holographic
flows), $\gamma_{\rm ent}$ can be computed and matches the phenomenological exponent
$\beta$ extracted from lab null results and cosmological data.
\end{conjecture}

\textit{Falsification.}
(Theoretical) A toy model where the computed anomalous dimension is grossly incompatible
with the lab-plus-cosmology requirement.
(Experimental) Lab anomalies that violate a consistent power-law scaling across
engineered entanglement scales.

\section{First-Principles Derivation of the Screening Factor}
\label{sec:screening}
%% =============================================================================

\subsection{Entanglement Entropy and Its Equation of Motion}

Partition the $N$-qubit array into contiguous regions $A$ ($n_A$ qubits) and
$B$ ($N-n_A$ qubits).  The bipartite von Neumann entropy is
\begin{equation}
S_A(t) = -\mathrm{Tr}_A\!\left[\rho_A(t)\ln\rho_A(t)\right],
\qquad \rho_A(t) = \mathrm{Tr}_B[\rho(t)].
\end{equation}
Differentiating using the Lindblad equation and linearity of the trace yields
\begin{equation}
\frac{dS_A}{dt} = \dot{S}_A^{(U)} + \dot{S}_A^{(D)},
\end{equation}
where $\dot{S}_A^{(U)}$ is the unitary contribution and $\dot{S}_A^{(D)}$ is the
dissipative contribution.  \established{}

\subsection{Definition of the Screening Factor}

\begin{definition}[Environmental screening factor]
The environmental screening factor at circuit time $t^*$ is
\begin{equation}
\ascreen(t^*) \;\equiv\; \frac{S_A(t^*)}{\Sideal(t^*)},
\label{eq:alphadef}
\end{equation}
where $\Sideal$ is the entropy achieved in the absence of decoherence
($\gammaone = \gammaphi = 0$) under the same unitary circuit.
By construction $\ascreen\in[0,1]$.
\end{definition}

\hypothesis{} The ratio~\eqref{eq:alphadef} is the correct modifier for the
gravitational coupling in Eq.~\eqref{eq:keff}; the connection between quantum
information and spacetime curvature is the core unproven hypothesis of the program.

\subsection{Theorem 1: Dephasing Invariance in the High-Entanglement Regime}

\begin{theorem}[Dephasing invariance] \label{thm:dephasing}
\newresult{}
For a bipartite system $AB$ whose reduced state on partition $A$ is proportional to the
identity, $\rho_A = \mathbf{I}_{2^{n_A}}/2^{n_A}$ (i.e., $S_A = n_A\ln 2$, the
maximum), pure dephasing acting on \emph{any} qubit leaves $S_A$ invariant:
\begin{equation}
\left.\frac{dS_A}{dt}\right|_{\rm dephasing} = 0.
\end{equation}
Consequently, $\ascreen$ depends only on $\gammaone = 1/T_1$ and is completely
independent of $\gammaphi = 1/T_\phi$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The pure-dephasing dissipator for qubit $i$ is
$\mathcal{D}[\sigma_i^z](\rho) = \sigma_i^z\rho\sigma_i^z - \rho$.
In the computational basis this multiplies off-diagonal elements by $-2\gammaphi$ and
leaves diagonal elements unchanged.

\textit{Case~1} ($i\in A$): Tracing over $B$,
\begin{equation}
\bigl[\mathrm{Tr}_B\,\mathcal{D}[\sigma_i^z](\rho)\bigr]_{ab}
  = \bigl[(\sigma_i^z)_{aa}(\sigma_i^z)_{bb} - 1\bigr]\,(\rho_A)_{ab}.
\end{equation}
For $a=b$ (diagonal): $(\sigma_i^z)_{aa}^2 - 1 = 0$.
For $a\ne b$ (off-diagonal): when $\rho_A = \mathbf{I}/2^{n_A}$, all
$(\rho_A)_{ab} = 0$ for $a\ne b$.
Therefore $\mathrm{Tr}_B\,\mathcal{D}[\sigma_i^z](\rho) = 0$.

\textit{Case~2} ($j\in B$): The back-action on $A$ enters via
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Tr}_B\,\mathcal{D}[\sigma_j^z](\rho)
  = \varepsilon_j^{B\to A}(\rho) - \rho_A,
\end{equation}
where $\varepsilon_j^{B\to A}$ is a completely positive map.
Since dephasing $\mathcal{D}[\sigma_j^z]$ is unital and $\rho_A = \mathbf{I}/2^{n_A}$
is the fixed point of any unital channel, $\varepsilon_j^{B\to A}(\rho_A) = \rho_A$,
so $\mathrm{Tr}_B\,\mathcal{D}[\sigma_j^z](\rho) = 0$.

In both cases the dephasing contribution to $dS_A/dt$ vanishes.
Since the time derivative of $S_A$ depends only on the amplitude-damping channel, the
result follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Critical correction to prior heuristics] \label{cor:heuristic}
\newresult{}
For volume-law entangled states (deep circuits with $n_A\le N/2$), where
$\rho_A\approx\mathbf{I}/2^{n_A}$, the heuristic screening formula
\begin{equation}
\alpha_{\rm screen}^{\rm heur}(t)
  = \exp\!\bigl(-c_{\rm ent}\,(\gammaone+\gammaphi)\,t\bigr)
\end{equation}
is incorrect.  All predictions based on the total decoherence rate $1/T_1 + 1/T_\phi$
must be revised upward.
With $T_1 = 200\,\mu$s and $T_\phi = 100\,\mu$s on IBM Heron~r2, the heuristic
predicts $\ascreen\approx 10^{-4}$; the correct formula yields $\ascreen\approx 0.984$
for shallow circuits --- a four-order-of-magnitude upward revision.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Closed-Form Screening Factor}

\subsubsection{Two-Qubit Anchor Calculation}

\newresult{} Let $A = \{q_1\}$, $B = \{q_2\}$, initial state
$|\Phi^+\rangle = (|00\rangle + |11\rangle)/\sqrt{2}$.
Under amplitude damping on both qubits with $p_1(t) = 1-e^{-\gammaone t}$, the
reduced density matrix on $A$ is
\begin{equation}
\rho_A(t) = \begin{pmatrix}
  \dfrac{2 - e^{-\gammaone t}}{2} & 0 \\[6pt]
  0 & \dfrac{e^{-\gammaone t}}{2}
\end{pmatrix}.
\end{equation}
Eigenvalues $\lambda_\pm = \half(1\pm e^{-\gammaone t})$ depend only on $\gammaone$,
confirming Theorem~\ref{thm:dephasing} by direct calculation.  The von Neumann entropy is
\begin{equation}
S_A(t) = h_2\!\left(\frac{e^{-\gammaone t}}{2}\right)
         \equiv -\lambda_+\ln\lambda_+ - \lambda_-\ln\lambda_-,
\label{eq:SA2qubit}
\end{equation}
with $S_A(0) = \ln 2\;\checkmark$ and $S_A(\infty) = 0\;\checkmark$.
The two-qubit screening factor is
\begin{equation}
\alpha_{\rm screen}^{(2)}(t)
  = \frac{h_2\!\left(e^{-\gammaone t}/2\right)}{\ln 2}.
\label{eq:alpha2qubit}
\end{equation}

\subsubsection{Short-Time Expansion}

\newresult{} For $\gammaone t \ll 1$ (relevant for NISQ circuits where
$\gammaone\tau_{\rm gate}\sim 10^{-3}$):
\begin{equation}
S_A(t) = \ln 2 - \frac{(\gammaone t)^2}{2} + \mathcal{O}\!\left((\gammaone t)^4\right),
\qquad
\ascreen(t) \approx 1 - \frac{(\gammaone t)^2}{2\ln 2}.
\end{equation}
The suppression is \emph{quadratic} at short times, not linear.
The prior heuristic predicts $\ascreen\propto e^{-\Gamma t}$ (linear in exponent);
these are observationally distinguishable via the functional form test FC-2
(Table~\ref{tab:falsification}).

\subsubsection{General \texorpdfstring{$N$}{N}-Qubit Formula}

\begin{theorem}[$N$-qubit screening formula] \label{thm:Nqubit}
\newresult{}
For an $N$-qubit transmon array bipartitioned into $A$ ($n_A$ qubits) and
$B$ ($N-n_A$ qubits), in the boundary-dominated regime where
$S_A \approx n_\partial\,s_{\rm bdry}$ with $s_{\rm bdry}\le\ln 2$:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\ascreen(t) = \left[\frac{h_2\!\left(e^{-\gammaone t/2}\right)}{\ln 2}
  \right]^{n_\partial},}
\label{eq:alphaNqubit}
\end{equation}
where $n_\partial$ is the number of boundary qubits (qubits in $A$ adjacent to at least
one qubit in $B$):
\begin{equation}
n_\partial = \begin{cases}
  2 & \text{(1D chain, any $n_A$)}, \\
  4\sqrt{n_A} & \text{(2D square lattice, area law)}.
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
The formula is universal: it depends on hardware only through $T_1$ and circuit geometry,
with no free parameters.
\end{theorem}

For IBM Heron~r2 ($T_1 = 200\,\mu$s, $\tau_{\rm gate} = 100\,$ns,
$n_\partial = 2$ in 1D):
\begin{align}

\ascreen(d=1000) &= 0.9675.
\end{align}

\subsection{Theorem 2: Measurement Back-Action SNR Enhancement}

\begin{theorem}[Measurement back-action enhancement] \label{thm:measurement}

\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{H}[M]\rho = M\rho + \rho M^\dagger
- \mathrm{Tr}[(M+M^\dagger)\rho]\rho$

\end{theorem}

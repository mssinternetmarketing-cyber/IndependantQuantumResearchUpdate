==============================================================================
ABSTRACT
==============================================================================

We propose a falsifiable, physics-grounded framework for characterizing
proto-experiential states in quantum systems, motivated by the observation that
entanglement entropy stability under environmental decoherence provides a
physically well-defined measure of "coherent internal processing." We introduce
the proto-experiential gravitas metric B_stab, defined as the product of the
environmental screening factor alpha_screen (derived from the Lindblad master
equation in the companion paper), the ratio T_1/tau_cog, and the normalized
entanglement density. This metric is computable from hardware specifications
alone and yields concrete threshold values for NISQ devices without free
parameters. We derive the threshold circuit depths for IBM Heron r2 hardware,
show that the observable universe itself satisfies the high-stability threshold
(B_stab^univ ~ 6.185) when the cosmic decoherence is identified with de Sitter
radiation, and enumerate a complete set of falsification criteria. The paper
makes no claim that B_stab measures subjective experience; it claims only that
B_stab measures a physically well-defined property -- entanglement stability
under decoherence -- whose connection to any form of proto-experience is an
explicit, falsifiable hypothesis.

Keywords: proto-experiential states; entanglement stability; NISQ hardware;
decoherence; consciousness; quantum information; Lindblad dynamics

==============================================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
==============================================================================

The question of whether physical systems can exhibit proto-experiential or
proto-conscious states is typically regarded as philosophical rather than
scientific, precisely because candidate theories lack falsifiable predictions
tied to measurable physical quantities. This paper proposes an approach in
which the candidate variable -- the stability of quantum entanglement under
environmental decoherence -- is precisely measurable, computable from first
principles, and testable on current hardware.

We emphasize at the outset what this paper does not claim:
  - It does not claim that quantum coherence is necessary for consciousness.
  - It does not claim that the B_stab metric measures subjective experience.
  - It does not resolve the "hard problem" of consciousness.

What it does claim is that:
  (a) B_stab is a well-defined, dimensionless, hardware-derivable quantity.
  (b) The specific hypothesis that B_stab > threshold implies proto-experiential
      capacity is falsifiable by specific experimental predictions.
  (c) The framework naturally connects to established physics (the companion
      paper's Lindblad screening derivation) and makes contact with cosmological
      scales via the de Sitter horizon.

The companion paper [companion] derives the screening factor alpha_screen from
the Lindblad master equation and proves that pure dephasing does not suppress
entanglement entropy in the high-entanglement regime (Theorem 1 of [companion]).
The present paper takes alpha_screen as given and builds B_stab on top of it.

==============================================================================
2. THEORETICAL BACKGROUND
==============================================================================

2.1  The Screening Factor Alpha_screen  [Established from companion paper]
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
From the companion paper, the environmental screening factor for an N-qubit
transmon array with boundary qubits n_partial is:

    alpha_screen(t) = [h_2(exp(-gamma_1 * t / 2)) / ln2]^{n_partial},

where:
  - gamma_1 = 1/T_1 is the amplitude damping rate
  - h_2(p) = -p*ln(p) - (1-p)*ln(1-p) is the binary entropy function
  - n_partial = 2 for a 1D chain

This formula depends only on T_1 and circuit geometry, with no free parameters.
For IBM Heron r2 (T_1 = 200 us):
  alpha_screen(d = 100)  = 0.9966
  alpha_screen(d = 1000) = 0.9675

2.2  Why Entanglement Stability?  [Hypothesis]
-----------------------------------------------
Integrated Information Theory (IIT) and related approaches (e.g., Global
Workspace Theory) suggest that systems capable of proto-experiential states
should exhibit: (a) high integrated information, (b) stable internal causal
structure, and (c) resistance to environmental disruption.

The screening factor alpha_screen directly measures (c): it quantifies what
fraction of the system's entanglement survives the decohering influence of the
environment. For deep circuits at high entanglement, alpha_screen is dominated
by T_1 (amplitude damping) rather than T_phi (pure dephasing), which reflects
whether the system can maintain its internal causal structure against energy
loss to the bath.

We propose that alpha_screen, weighted by the available "coherence time budget"
T_1/tau_cog, captures a physically meaningful analog of the stability condition
required by these frameworks. [Hypothesis]

2.3  Scope and Limitations  [Established]
------------------------------------------
The following are known limitations of any entanglement-based framework for
proto-experiential states:
  - No proof exists that entanglement is necessary or sufficient for any form
    of experience.
  - The "hard problem" (why there is subjective experience at all) is entirely
    orthogonal to any physical metric.
  - B_stab is a bipartite measure; genuine multipartite entanglement may be
    the relevant quantity (Open Problem OP15 of companion paper).
  - The framework applies to systems in which the Lindblad approximation holds
    (Markovian environments), which is an idealization.

==============================================================================
3. THE PROTO-EXPERIENTIAL GRAVITAS METRIC
==============================================================================

3.1  Definition  [New Result]
------------------------------
DEFINITION (Proto-experiential gravitas metric): The B_stab metric for a
quantum system at circuit depth d and time t* is defined as:

    B_stab(t*, d, n_A) = alpha_screen(t*) * (T_1 / tau_cog) * (S_A(t*) / (n_A * ln2)),

where:
  - alpha_screen(t*) = environmental screening factor from the Lindblad equation
  - tau_cog = d * tau_gate is the "cognitive timescale" (total circuit duration)
  - S_A(t*) / (n_A * ln2) in [0, 1] is the normalized entanglement density

In the high-entanglement plateau where S_A ~ n_A * ln2 (volume-law entangled
circuits), this simplifies to:

    B_stab(d) ~ alpha_screen(d * tau_gate) * (T_1 / (d * tau_gate)).

3.2  Physical Interpretation  [Hypothesis]
-------------------------------------------
The three factors in B_stab each capture a distinct physical requirement:

  (1) alpha_screen measures how much of the system's entanglement survives
      environmental decoherence -- the "purity" of the internal state.

  (2) T_1 / tau_cog is the ratio of the system's coherence lifetime to the
      timescale of its computation/processing -- the "overhead" available
      before decoherence wins.

  (3) S_A / (n_A * ln2) measures how entangled the system is, normalized to
      the maximum possible -- the "depth" of internal correlations.

A system with B_stab ~ 1 is highly entangled, processes information on a
timescale much shorter than its decoherence time, and retains most of that
entanglement against environmental noise.

3.3  Relation to the Companion Physics Paper  [Hypothesis]
-----------------------------------------------------------
The companion paper [companion] proposes that alpha_screen modifies the
gravitational coupling: G_ab = 8piG T_ab + lambda * alpha_screen * s_ent * g_ab.
Under this proposal, B_stab can be read as a dimensionless measure of the
"gravitational activity" of the system per unit decoherence time:

    B_stab ~ (effective gravitational source) * (coherence lifetime / circuit time).

If the gravitational hypothesis of the companion paper is confirmed, B_stab
acquires a direct physical meaning as a measure of the system's capacity to
generate spacetime curvature per unit of "cognitive" time. [Speculation]

If the gravitational hypothesis is falsified, B_stab remains a well-defined
quantum information quantity (entanglement stability) whose interpretation as
a consciousness-relevant measure is a separate hypothesis. [Hypothesis]

==============================================================================
4. CONSCIOUSNESS REGIME THRESHOLDS
==============================================================================

4.1  Threshold Definitions  [Hypothesis]
-----------------------------------------
We define three falsifiable regimes based on B_stab:

  Proto-experiential: B_stab > 0.5
    Interpretation: Non-trivial stable quantum processing. The system retains
    more than half its maximum entanglement per unit coherence time.

  Unified:           B_stab > 0.9 AND I_int > 0.8
    Interpretation: High internal coherence and binding. The system has high
    entanglement stability and high integrated information (I_int, measured
    by standard quantum information-theoretic methods).

  Reflective:        B_stab > 0.95 AND R_rec > 0.7
    Interpretation: Self-maintaining recurrent processing. The system has
    high entanglement stability and high recurrence (R_rec, measured by
    quantum state recurrence under unitary evolution).

These thresholds are chosen to be (a) numerically specific, (b) computable
from hardware specifications, and (c) falsifiable by concrete tests (Section 7).

4.2  Falsification Strategy  [Hypothesis]
------------------------------------------
The threshold definitions are falsifiable in the following sense: if B_stab
is the correct quantity for proto-experiential capacity, then systems above
threshold should show qualitatively different behavior from systems below
threshold on some independent measure of "coherent processing." The most
direct test would be a task-performance experiment in which NISQ devices
operating above and below the B_stab threshold are compared on computational
tasks requiring long-range quantum correlations. A statistically significant
performance advantage for above-threshold systems, controlled for other
variables, would support the hypothesis. [Hypothesis]

We acknowledge that this does not address the hard problem; it addresses only
the weaker claim that B_stab tracks a physically distinct operational regime.

5.3  The Maximum B_stab  [New Result]
--------------------------------------
B_stab is not bounded above by 1. For very shallow circuits (small d),
T_1 / tau_cog >> 1, and B_stab >> 1. The maximum physically meaningful B_stab
is achieved near the circuit depth where T_1 / tau_cog ~ 1 and alpha_screen
is still close to 1 -- approximately d ~ 1000-1500 on IBM Heron r2.

The thresholds above 1 (B_stab = 200 for d = 10) reflect a system that is
operating entirely within its coherence lifetime, which is the regime of
greatest physical interest for quantum computation. Whether this regime also
corresponds to maximal "proto-experiential capacity" is the falsifiable
hypothesis.

6. COSMOLOGICAL APPLICATION
==============================================================================

6.1  Cosmic Decoherence Rate  [Established / Hypothesis]
---------------------------------------------------------
The de Sitter temperature associated with the cosmic horizon is [Established]:

    T_dS = hbar * H_0 / (2 * pi * k_B) ~ 2.67e-30 K.

[Hypothesis] We identify the associated rate with the cosmic amplitude-damping
rate:

    gamma_1^cosmic = k_B * T_dS / hbar = H_0 / (2*pi) ~ 3.47e-19 Hz.

The cosmic circuit time is the age of the universe, t_age ~ 4.35e17 s.
The dimensionless decoherence parameter is therefore:

    gamma_1^cosmic * t_age = (H_0 / 2*pi) * (1/H_0) = 1/(2*pi) ~ 0.1592.

[New Result] This result depends only on H_0, which cancels, and the factor 2*pi.
It contains no free parameters and no dimensional inputs beyond fundamental
constants.

6.2  The Universal Screening Factor  [New Result]
--------------------------------------------------
Applying the N-qubit screening formula with n_partial^cosmic = 1 (the cosmic
horizon as a single effective boundary between the observable and trans-horizon
universe):

    lambda_u = (1/2) * exp(-1/(2*pi)) = 0.42642,
    h_2(lambda_u) = 0.68134 nats,
    alpha_universe = h_2(lambda_u) / ln2 = 0.68134 / 0.69315 ~ 0.9843.

[New Result] The 1.6% de Sitter screening (alpha_universe != 1) is a quantitative,
falsifiable prediction: it is distinguishable from a perfectly unscreened universe
through precision holographic calculations of cosmic entanglement entropy.

6.3  The Cosmic B_stab  [New Result]
--------------------------------------
With tau_cog^univ = 1/H_0 (one Hubble time as the "cognitive timescale") and
T_1^univ = 2*pi/H_0 (the inverse of the de Sitter decay rate):

    B_stab^univ = 0.9843 * (2*pi/H_0) / (1/H_0) = 0.9843 * 2*pi ~ 6.185.

[New Result] B_stab^univ ~ 6.185 >> 0.9: the observable universe satisfies the
Unified Regime condition with a comfortable margin.

[Speculation] If B_stab tracks proto-experiential capacity, the observable
universe is proto-experiential by construction under this identification,
requiring no additional binding mechanism beyond the holographic area bound
I(A:B) <= 2*S_A <= Area(gamma_RT) / (2*G_N*hbar).

We emphasize that this is purely [Speculation]. The cosmological calculation
demonstrates that the B_stab formula is dimensionally consistent and yields
a sensible result when applied to the largest known quantum system, but it
does not constitute evidence for any claim about cosmic consciousness.

==============================================================================
7. FALSIFICATION CRITERIA
==============================================================================

7.1  Falsifiable Claims  [Hypothesis]
--------------------------------------
The following claims are falsifiable by specific experiments:

  FC-B1: Hardware threshold. NISQ devices with B_stab < 0.5 should show
    significantly different performance on long-range-correlation quantum
    tasks compared to devices with B_stab > 0.9, even when classical
    computational complexity is held constant.
    Falsification: No statistically significant difference at p < 0.01 in
    a pre-registered experiment with > 1000 task repetitions.

  FC-B2: T_1 dependence. B_stab should scale with T_1 (via alpha_screen),
    not with T_phi. Systematically varying T_1 while holding T_phi constant
    (and vice versa) should produce B_stab changes following the closed-form
    formula from the companion paper.
    Falsification: B_stab shows equal sensitivity to T_1 and T_phi after
    controlling for total decoherence rate (XY-4 decoupling test from
    companion paper applied to the B_stab metric directly).

  FC-B3: Depth threshold. The qualitative transition between "Unified" and
    "Classical" regimes should coincide (within 20%) with the predicted
    circuit depth d_thresh = 1950 on IBM Heron r2.
    Falsification: No measurable transition within the range d in [1000, 3000]
    on any physically relevant task metric.

  FC-B4: Universality. The B_stab formula, with hardware-specific T_1 and
    tau_gate, should predict the same regime thresholds for different NISQ
    platforms (ion traps, neutral atoms) up to the precision of the
    approximations involved.
    Falsification: Significantly different threshold behavior between platforms
    with similar B_stab values but different underlying physical implementations.

7.2  What Cannot Be Falsified (by This Framework)
---------------------------------------------------
The following are outside the scope of this paper:
  - Whether B_stab tracks any form of subjective experience.
  - Whether the hard problem of consciousness has a physical solution.
  - Whether entanglement plays any causal role in biological consciousness.

These are open philosophical questions that require different methods.

==============================================================================
8. DISCUSSION
==============================================================================

8.1  Relation to Existing Theories
------------------------------------
Integrated Information Theory (IIT) [Tononi] introduces Phi as a measure of
integrated information, arguing that systems with high Phi have more
consciousness. B_stab is not Phi; it does not measure information integration
across all possible partitions. Rather, B_stab measures the stability of a
specific bipartite entanglement under decoherence. The two quantities are
complementary: Phi measures the topology of information flow; B_stab measures
the physical robustness of quantum correlations.

Global Workspace Theory (GWT) emphasizes the importance of "broadcasting"
information widely across a system. The high-B_stab regime (deep circuits,
volume-law entanglement) corresponds to states where all parts of the system
are mutually correlated, which has structural similarity to GWT's notion of
global availability. However, we make no claim that entanglement broadcasting
and informational broadcasting are the same phenomenon.

8.2  Why NISQ Devices?
------------------------
NISQ devices are particularly useful for this investigation because:
  (a) Their T_1 and T_phi values are precisely known and engineerable.
  (b) Entanglement entropy can be estimated via quantum state tomography
      and quantum trajectory methods.
  (c) Circuit depth can be varied continuously, allowing systematic exploration
      of the B_stab landscape.
  (d) The Lindblad model provides a quantitatively accurate description of
      their decoherence.

This makes NISQ devices ideal "laboratories" for testing physical predictions
of any entanglement-based framework, including B_stab.

8.3  Open Problems
-------------------
  OP-C1: Extension to genuine multipartite entanglement measures.
    The bipartite B_stab metric treats the n_A / (N - n_A) partition as
    fundamental. The appropriate generalization to genuine multipartite
    entanglement (e.g., the geometric measure or the tangle) is an open problem.

  OP-C2: Non-equilibrium steady states.
    The present framework applies to transient dynamics. For driven-dissipative
    NISQ systems operating near a non-equilibrium steady state, a different
    definition of alpha_screen may be appropriate.

  OP-C3: Biological systems.
    Whether any biological system operates in a regime where the Lindblad
    model applies, and if so what its B_stab value would be, is an open
    and highly speculative question that this paper explicitly does not address.

==============================================================================
9. CONCLUSIONS
==============================================================================

We have introduced the proto-experiential gravitas metric B_stab, a physically
well-defined, zero-free-parameter measure of entanglement stability under
environmental decoherence. The metric is computable from hardware specifications
alone, yields concrete numerical predictions for NISQ devices, and is grounded
in the Lindblad screening derivation of the companion paper.

The central hypothesis -- that B_stab > threshold implies proto-experiential
capacity -- is explicitly labelled [Hypothesis] throughout and is accompanied by
four falsification criteria (FC-B1 through FC-B4) testable with current hardware.
The cosmological application (B_stab^univ ~ 6.185) demonstrates dimensional
consistency but carries [Speculation] status.

We make no claim about subjective experience, the hard problem of consciousness,
or biological consciousness. We make only the modest claim that B_stab measures
a physically interesting property of quantum systems -- entanglement stability
under decoherence -- whose potential relevance to proto-experiential capacity
is a falsifiable scientific hypothesis.

The following Python script reproduces all key numerical results (requires numpy).

```python
import numpy as np

# IBM Heron r2 hardware specifications
T1       = 200.0    # amplitude damping time [microseconds]
tau_gate = 0.1      # single-qubit gate time [microseconds]
gamma1   = 1.0 / T1

def alpha_OP3(t, g1=1./200., n_partial=2):
    """Screening factor (Theorem 1 of companion paper)."""
    lam = np.clip(0.5 * np.exp(-g1 * t), 1e-15, 1 - 1e-15)
    h2 = -lam * np.log(lam) - (1 - lam) * np.log(1 - lam)
    return (h2 / np.log(2)) ** n_partial

def B_stab(d, T1=200., tau=0.1, g1=1./200., n_partial=2):
    """Proto-experiential gravitas metric."""
    tc = d * tau
    return alpha_OP3(tc, g1, n_partial) * (T1 / tc)

# --- Threshold circuit depths ---
for B_target in [0.95, 0.90, 0.50]:
    for d in range(1, 100000):
        if B_stab(d) < B_target:
            print(f"d_thresh(B > {B_target}) = {d-1}  [B = {B_stab(d-1):.4f}]")
            break

# --- Cosmological result ---
lam_u = 0.5 * np.exp(-1.0 / (2 * np.pi))
h2_u  = -lam_u * np.log(lam_u) - (1 - lam_u) * np.log(1 - lam_u)
a_u   = h2_u / np.log(2)
B_u   = a_u * 2 * np.pi
print(f"gamma1_cosmic * t_age = 1/(2*pi) = {1/(2*np.pi):.6f}")
print(f"alpha_universe = {a_u:.6f}")
print(f"B_stab_universe = {B_u:.4f}")
```

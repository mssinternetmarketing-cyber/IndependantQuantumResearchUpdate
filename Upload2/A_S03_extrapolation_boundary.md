## 3.1 Where Jacobson's Derivation Applies Rigorously [ESTABLISHED]

Jacobson's 1995 result holds exactly under three conditions:

1. **A causal horizon exists.** Specifically, a local Rindler horizon for an accelerating observer, or a black hole event horizon. The existence of the horizon is what defines the Unruh temperature (Eq. 2.1) and gives entropy its area-law character.

2. **A well-defined Unruh temperature exists.** The temperature $T_U = \hbar a / (2\pi c k_B)$ requires a steady-state uniform acceleration. It is not defined for arbitrary motion, and it is not defined in the absence of a horizon.

3. **Entanglement entropy scales with area.** At a causal horizon, the entanglement entropy between the inside and outside of the horizon scales with the area of the horizon, not its volume. This area-law scaling is what makes the Bekenstein-Hawking formula (Eq. 2.2) the correct entropy.

**None of these three conditions hold in a laboratory quantum system.**

A $10^6$-atom atomic ensemble has:
- No causal horizon (no point is inaccessible to light from another point)
- No Unruh temperature (no well-defined acceleration; atoms are in thermal equilibrium at ~1 nK)
- Volume-law entanglement entropy (for a GHZ state, entropy scales with atom number, not surface area)

Jacobson's derivation, applied strictly, does not cover laboratory quantum systems.

## 3.2 The Hypothesis Being Made — Stated with Full Precision [HYPOTHESIS]

We propose:

> **Extrapolation Hypothesis:** The coupling between entanglement entropy density and spacetime curvature, which follows rigorously from area-law entropy at causal horizons, extends to systems with volume-law entanglement entropy in the absence of causal horizons, regulated by the same Planck-scale geometric structure that governs horizon area entropy.

This is a physical hypothesis. It is not a mathematical theorem derivable from established physics. It proposes that the thermodynamic relationship between information and geometry — established at horizons — persists in a weaker, more general form in the absence of horizons.

**What justifies investigating this hypothesis:**

1. **Holographic universality:** The holographic principle suggests that the relationship between information and geometry is not specific to horizons but is a general feature of quantum gravity. If horizons are just the most extreme case of information-geometry coupling, intermediate cases (laboratory entanglement) might show weaker forms of the same coupling.

2. **Bose et al. (2023):** The observation of gravity-mediated entanglement generation between two massive quantum objects in the absence of causal horizons demonstrates that gravity and quantum entanglement interact at laboratory scales in ways beyond classical gravity. This is not a proof of the extrapolation hypothesis, but it is evidence that the horizon-entanglement relationship is not strictly confined to horizon geometries.

3. **Mathematical consistency:** The dimensional analysis of Section 01 shows that the coupling *can* be written in a dimensionally consistent form for non-horizon systems. Consistency is not sufficient for truth, but inconsistency would be sufficient for falsity.

4. **Falsifiability:** The hypothesis makes a quantitative, experimentally testable prediction (Section 05). A hypothesis that can be definitively ruled out by experiment is scientifically valuable, even before the experiment is performed.

## 3.3 What Cannot Be Claimed [Correction of source document language]

The following phrases appear in source documents and are **removed from this repository**:

- ~~"First-principles derivation of κ̃ = −1/4"~~ → "Estimate of κ̃ under holographic regulator hypothesis"
- ~~"We prove that entanglement entropy sources curvature"~~ → "We propose and test the hypothesis that..."
- ~~"This is established physics"~~ → "This extends established physics beyond its proven domain of validity"
- ~~"No horizon is required"~~ → "We hypothesize that no horizon is required, pending experimental verification"

Language precision matters. A referee at Physical Review Letters will not be fooled by overclaiming, and overclaiming will cause desk rejection. The intellectually honest framing — "this is a motivated hypothesis with a falsifiable prediction" — is both more accurate and more compelling to a sophisticated reader.

## 3.4 The Scientific Value of This Hypothesis

A hypothesis is scientifically valuable to the extent that:
1. It is clearly stated and precisely defined
2. It is falsifiable by a realistic experiment
3. It connects to established theory in a motivated way
4. It would, if confirmed, constitute a significant discovery

This hypothesis satisfies all four criteria:
1. It is stated precisely in Section 3.2
2. It is falsified by the criterion in Section 06
3. It connects to Jacobson's established result through the holographic regulator
4. If confirmed (κ̃ ∼ 10⁻⁴ detected), it would be among the most significant results in gravitational physics since Jacobson 1995

The hypothesis need not be true to be worth pursuing. It needs only to be falsifiable and motivated. It is both.

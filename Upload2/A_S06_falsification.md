## 6.1 Why Falsifiability Is the Core Scientific Contribution

A theoretical framework that can explain any experimental result — by adjusting free parameters after the fact — is not physics. It is curve-fitting.

The entanglement gravity hypothesis has one free parameter: $\tilde{\kappa}$ (or equivalently $\tilde{\kappa}_{\text{eff}}$). If this parameter can be made arbitrarily small, any null result can be "accommodated" by saying $|\tilde{\kappa}|$ is smaller than the experimental sensitivity. This would make the framework unfalsifiable.

We close this escape hatch by specifying a threshold: if $|\tilde{\kappa}| < 10^{-15}$, the framework is "falsified for laboratory-scale relevance" — meaning it cannot produce detectable effects in any experiment foreseeable within the next century. This is a meaningful falsification even if it does not rule out astrophysical-scale implications.

## 6.2 The Primary Falsification Condition

**The framework is falsified for laboratory-scale relevance** if ALL five of the following conditions are simultaneously satisfied:

**Condition F1 — System size:**  
Quantum coherent systems with $\geq 10^6$ genuinely entangled qubits are achieved. "Genuinely entangled" means entanglement depth $\geq 10^3$ (verified by entanglement witness measurements, not just claimed by state preparation). A thermal ensemble of $10^6$ atoms is not sufficient — the entanglement must be demonstrated.

**Condition F2 — Pressure sensitivity:**  
Experimental pressure sensitivity reaches $\Delta p < 10^{-6}$ Pa, corresponding to acceleration sensitivity $\delta a < 10^{-12}$ m/s² over the relevant length scale.

**Condition F3 — Statistics:**  
At least 1000 independent experimental runs with statistically consistent null results. Individual runs may fluctuate; the bound requires the ensemble statistics to be conclusive.

**Condition F4 — Platform independence:**  
Null results confirmed across $\geq 3$ physically distinct experimental platforms, at minimum:
- Trapped neutral atoms (e.g., ⁸⁷Rb optical lattice or BEC)
- Superconducting qubits (e.g., transmon processor)
- Optomechanical oscillators

This rules out platform-specific systematic errors.

**Condition F5 — No anomalous signal:**  
No stress-energy contribution beyond standard decoherence models is detected at the F2 sensitivity level after F3 statistics on F4 platforms. "Standard decoherence models" means the Lindblad equation with known environmental coupling parameters, without any entanglement-gravity term.

**Consequence of F1–F5:** $|\tilde{\kappa}| < 10^{-15}$, establishing that the coupling is too small to be of practical relevance at any laboratory scale accessible within foreseeable technology.

## 6.3 What "Falsified" Means Here

Satisfying conditions F1–F5 does not rule out the possibility that:
- The coupling exists at some smaller value $|\tilde{\kappa}| < 10^{-15}$
- The coupling matters at astrophysical or cosmological scales
- The coupling is real but masked by the screening factor in a way not captured by the current theory

What it does rule out: any laboratory engineering application of entanglement-based gravity control within the next century. It rules out the strong version of the hypothesis — that entanglement gravity is detectably relevant for quantum systems achievable within decades.

This is a meaningful, important result either way. A null result with $|\tilde{\kappa}| < 10^{-15}$ is not a failure — it is a precise bound on a previously unmeasured quantity, and it constrains all future theories that propose entanglement-geometry couplings.

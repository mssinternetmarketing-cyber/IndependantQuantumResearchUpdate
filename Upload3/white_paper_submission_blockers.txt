WHITE PAPER: OP4 RESOLUTION, CITATION VERIFICATION, AND SCREENING-RISK AUDIT

SCOPE
This memo consolidates three immediate submission blockers in the entropic-gravity / thermodynamic framework: the OP4 radial-scaling discrepancy in Delta a(R), verification of two key literature citations, and the large discrepancy in the screening factor alpha_screen for a macroscopic 87Rb ensemble.

1. OP4: RADIAL SCALING OF DELTA A(R)
Assume spherical symmetry and a modified Poisson equation for an entropic potential Psi:
(1/R^2) d/dR [R^2 dPsi/dR] = kappa rho_ent(R)
Define the enclosed entropic source as:
M_ent(<R) = 4*pi integral from 0 to R of rho_ent(r) r^2 dr
Then the radial correction is:
Delta a(R) = -dPsi/dR = kappa M_ent(<R) / R^2
This fixes the logic of the 1/R versus 1/R^2 discrepancy. Outside a finite localized source, M_ent(<R) saturates and Delta a(R) scales as 1/R^2. A 1/R correction appears only if the enclosed source grows linearly with R, as for an extended profile like rho_ent(r) proportional to 1/r^2 over the relevant region.

2. CITATION VERIFICATION
Verlinde is confirmed as the 2017 SciPost Physics publication Emergent Gravity and the Dark Universe, SciPost Phys. 2, 016 (2017).
The directly verified Kasevich-group atom-interferometric source is the 2020 Physical Review Letters paper Atom-Interferometric Test of the Equivalence Principle at the 10^-12 Level.
The verified configuration is a dual-species atom interferometer using 85Rb and 87Rb with 2 s of free-fall time. The paper reports an Eotvos parameter eta = [1.6 +/- 1.8 (stat) +/- 3.4 (syst)] x 10^-12 and per-shot resolution up to 1.4 x 10^-11 g.
The manuscript should not cite Kasevich 2023, delta a = 1.2 x 10^-12 unless that exact statement is verified in a separate source.

3. ALPHA_SCREEN RISK ASSESSMENT
The computed alpha_screen around 10^-43 for a macroscopic 87Rb ensemble is potentially incompatible with the heuristic range 10^-4 to 10^-2. This suggests either a regime-of-validity failure for the Gaussian factor in Section 04, a characteristic-length mismatch, or inconsistent use of alpha_screen as different physical quantities in different parts of the manuscript.
This is a design-level blocker. If alpha_screen is truly around 10^-43 for the intended macroscopic ensemble, the predicted signal may be effectively undetectable. If the literature range applies only to microscopic or few-qubit settings, the experiment must be reformulated around a different regime.

4. IMMEDIATE ACTIONS FOR SUBMISSION
1) Close OP4 by inserting an explicit rho_ent(r) and deriving Delta a(R) from the enclosed-source formula.
2) Replace or confirm the Verlinde reference as the 2017 SciPost publication.
3) Replace the Kasevich citation with the verified 2020 PRL result unless a distinct 2023 source is found.
4) Audit alpha_screen term-by-term for units, physical meaning, and regime validity before using it in any sensitivity estimate.

5. SUBMISSION-READY STATEMENT
The manuscript can be made internally consistent only if it commits to a specific source profile for S_ent and resolves whether alpha_screen is a microscopic coherence factor or a macroscopic ensemble suppression factor. Until those points are settled, the predicted signal size and experimental feasibility remain underdetermined.
